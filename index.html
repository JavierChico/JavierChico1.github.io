Hi, I'm Javier Chico Vazquez.

A
I'm a Mathematics graduate from Imperial College London. My MSc thesis deals with Electromagnetic Fluid Dynamics, under the supervision of Demetrios Papageorgiou.

My research interest lie on a range of Applied topics, from theoretical Fluid Dynamics to Computational Partial Differential Equations
I particularly enjoy mathematical modelling in general, the weirder the topic the more exciting. It is fascinating to me how the language of mathematics can help us explain a wide range of phenomena, sometimes we can just translate a model from physics to say probability almost entirely, and it still works (all models are wrong at the end of the day)

In the fall, I will start my doctoral thesis at the Mathematical Institute, Oxford, under the supervision of Dominic Vella and Derek Moulton.

Blog and Personal Interests

In my free time I enjoy the Great Outdoors, sports, visiting and exploring new places. I have travelled as far West as British Columbia, as far East as Japan, as far North as Alesund and as far South as Johannesburg.

I also enjoy challenging myself with grueling hikes and demanding cycling routes surrounded by nature's paradise.
I have ascended many of the famous Cols from Le Tour de France, from cult classics such as Galibier and Tourmalet, as well as less known jewels like Iseran and Gavarnie.
As part of my research I try to connect my hobbies and interests with Mathematics, using them as inspiration for my models and trying to answer relevant questions mathematically (i.e. see my work on pelotons).

Apart from that, I am also an animal lover, a zoologist at heart. I am interested in quantitatively understanding animal-human interactions, habitat destruction and biodiversity loss. In conjuction with climate change, we are experiencing one of the most perilous times for life on Earth since the CretaceousPaleogene extinction event, and I believe Mathematics can play a leading role in healing our increasingly ailing planet.
